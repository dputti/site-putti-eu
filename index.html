Our Origins

From Italy to Brazil

The history of the Putti family traces back to the XI-XII century. The name is derived from Latin variations referring to "boy" or "teenager," symbolizing a youthful spirit that has endured through generations.

Originating from the Veneto region in Italy, our journey to the New World began in the late 19th century. Luigi Putti, a courageous patriarch, departed his homeland to seek new opportunities in the lush lands of Brazil.

1889

Luigi Putti arrives in Brazil on April 4, 1889, at the age of 22, destined for Alfredo Chaves (now Veranópolis), Rio Grande do Sul.

Ancient Roots & Research

The Putti name (historically also "de Putti") has deep roots in Northern Italy, flourishing in regions like Emilia (Ferrara), Veneto, and Liguria. Historical records identify early noble figures such as Dalila de Putti (born in Ferrara, 1484).

For those interested in tracing specific ancestors, public registers are invaluable. Official birth, marriage, and death records can be explored through:

  • Portale Antenati: The Italian National Archives' digital portal for civil records.
  • Comune di Adria: Local archives in Adria may hold specific records for Luigi Putti's direct lineage.
